About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at Xi'an University of Architecture and Technology. My research focuses on robot mechanism design, elastic actuation, control, and experimental validation, especially for legged robots and bio-inspired robotic systems.

I am particularly interested in how mechanical design, actuation, and control can be integrated into high-performance robotic platforms, including systems developed under limited-resource conditions. My work emphasizes real hardware prototypes, system-level integration, and experimentally validated results.

Research Interests

  • Legged robot mechanism and actuation design
  • Parallel elastic actuation and explosive robotic motion
  • Bio-inspired robotic locomotion and segmented robots
  • Robot control and mechanism-control co-design
  • Low-cost robotic prototyping and experimentally validated systems

Selected Publications

Parallel Elastic Ankle Actuator With a Unidirectional Clutch for Explosive Motion in Legged Robots
Dou Mingyuan et al. · IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters (RA-L), 2026

This work proposes a parallel elastic ankle actuator with a unidirectional clutch to decouple high-torque energy storage from rapid joint release, addressing the conflict among explosive output, energy efficiency, and joint mobility in legged robots.

  • Unidirectional-clutch-based decoupled parallel elastic actuation
  • Focused on explosive locomotion and high-performance ankle behavior
  • Validated through hardware experiments on a real robotic platform
Paper DOI Video
Caterpillar-Inspired Multi-Gait Generation Method for a Series-Parallel Hybrid Segmented Robot
Dou Mingyuan et al. · Biomimetics, 2024

This work presents a caterpillar-inspired segmented robot and a multi-gait generation method for adaptive locomotion. It explores how bio-inspired body structure and gait planning can improve robotic mobility in constrained and irregular terrain.

  • Bio-inspired segmented robot with series-parallel hybrid structure
  • Multiple gait modes for terrain adaptability
  • Experimentally validated locomotion performance
Paper DOI Video
Design and Implementation of a Novel Two-Wheeled Composite Self-Balancing Robot for Stationary Operations in Unknown Terrain
Co-author · IEEE Access, 2025

This work develops a two-wheeled composite self-balancing robot for stable stationary operations under uncertain terrain conditions, integrating mechanism design, balancing control, and extensive real-world evaluation.

  • Composite self-balancing robot platform
  • Robust performance on unknown terrain
